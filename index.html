Habitat UI & API

A full-stack environmental data platform built at UCBoulder.

This is an app made for the local Gunnison communnity to help combat the spread of Cheat Grass. HabitatUI is a React/TypeScript front-end for visualizing ecological survey data. It consumes REST endpoints exposed by HabitatApi, a Node.js/Express service backed by PostgreSQL. Together they allow researchers to:

  • Interactive maps: Filter, pan & zoom sensor and survey data
  • Real-time updates: WebSocket feeds for live environmental readings
  • Secure access: JWT-based auth via HabitatApi middleware
  • Deployable: Dockerized for Kubernetes or standalone use

Battle of Wits: Examining the Deceitful and Trustworthy Behaviors of LLMs

Apr 2025 - Apr 2025 | University of Colorado Boulder

In Battle of Wits, we introduce a two-agent social-deduction game, inspired by The Princess Bride, to probe when and how large language models (LLMs) choose to trust or deceive, and to uncover the reasoning behind those decisions. We repurpose a ‚Äúcups of poison‚Äù setup into a controlled ‚Äúboxes and block‚Äù scenario and evaluate a 7 billion-parameter model (gemma:7b) under zero-, one-, and two-shot prompting. By assigning explicit truth-telling or deceitful roles to each agent, we measure block-location guessing accuracy across the 2√ó2 belief‚Äìdisposition matrix.

  • ü§ñ Deceit Conditions: Deceit emerges reliably only when prompt examples and deceptive intent align, with one- and two-shot prompts significantly boosting the defender‚Äôs ability to mislead.
  • üîç Mixed Responses: Even with a deceitful mandate, LLMs sometimes default to truthful or mixed responses, yielding near-50 % baseline accuracy.
  • ‚ùì Zero-Shot Insight: Zero-shot prompting exhibits an inverse relationship between question count and guess accuracy, suggesting that simpler interrogation can outperform more elaborate questioning without examples.
